About Dr Mikhail Mastakov

Bariatric, Weight Loss and General Surgeon

Dr Mikhail Y. Mastakov MD, FRACS — Weight Loss & General Surgeon, Laparoscopic Emergency & Elective

Professional Background

Dr Mikhail Mastakov is a Bariatric (Obesity) and General Surgeon currently practising at Sunnybank Private Hospital, Mater Private Hospital Redland, Gold Coast Private Hospital and St Stephen's Hospital Hervey Bay.

Dr Mastakov specialises in:

  • Bariatric (Obesity) Surgery
  • Sleeve Gastrectomy and Gastric Bypass
  • Revision Bariatric Surgery
  • Hiatus and Abdominal Hernia Repair
  • Surgery for Gallstones and General Surgery
  • Laparoscopic emergency and elective care

Qualifications and Affiliations

Dr Mastakov is:

  • A Fellow of the Royal Australasian College of Surgeons (FRACS)
  • A member of General Surgeons Australia (GSA)
  • A member of the Australian & New Zealand Metabolic and Obesity Surgery Society (ANZMOSS)
  • Accredited for colonoscopy with GESA (Gastroenterological Society of Australia)

He has completed a preceptorship in Bariatric (Obesity) surgery with leading Australian bariatric surgeons practising in Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth and Adelaide.

Clinical Experience

Dr Mastakov completed his fellowship in Australia and has more than 28 years of experience in general surgery, procedures and surgical care for both public and private patients, including 17 years of surgery in Queensland hospitals.

His experience includes work at:

  • Princess Alexandra Hospital
  • QEII Jubilee Hospital
  • Logan Hospital
  • Redland Hospital
  • Townsville Teaching Hospital
  • Rockhampton and Nambour Base Hospitals

Surgical Approach

Laparoscopic and Open Surgery

Dr Mastakov is a laparoscopic surgeon. However, open rather than laparoscopic surgery may still be required in some conditions.

Laparoscopic surgery uses specialised equipment and smaller incisions, often called keyhole surgery. This surgical technique can offer benefits such as:

  • Smaller scars
  • Reduced post-operative pain
  • Shorter hospital stays
  • Quicker recovery times

These benefits apply where the approach is suitable for the patient and condition.
